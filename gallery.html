MANGOCHI, Malawi

Mangochi was developed as an agricultural centre and has marine-engineering shops. Cah crops grown in the area include tobacco, cotton and groundnuts, rice and maize are intensively grown along the lakeshore and commercial fishing is also important. Amenities include sevral shops, supermarkets, a post-office and banks.

The lake Malawi Museum houses ethnic, environmental and historical exhits. They include Hotchkiss gun gun which the British gunboat Gwendolen defeated the German gunman Hermann von Wissmann with a single shot in their brief naval engagement in August 1914. The meseum has also a scale model of the Gwendolen. An even older exhibit is a marine steam engine that was built in 1898 and powered the Universities' Mission to Centeral Africa's SS Chauncy Maples until 1953. Mangochi is home to a large mosque [3] as well as a moden Catholic Cathedral.

LENGWE NATIONAL PARK

Lengwe National Park located near the town of Chikwawa, about 40 miles southwest of Blantyre, Lengwe's topography is unusual for Malawi and consist of open deciduous forests and dense thickest. In Lengwe you will likely see Nyala antelope, a spiral-horned antelope native to southern Africa. Other animals that live in the park are giraffe, Spotted Hyena, Warthog, Kudu, Impala, Samango Monkey, Reedbuck, Cape Buffalo and Suni.

Interesting birds particularly; Shrikes, Rolles, Bee-eaters and Yellow-Spotted Nicator.

MULANJE

Mulanje, formerly called Mlanje, is a town in the southern Region of Malawi, close to the border with Mozambique, to the east. it is near the Mulanje Massif. Mulanje is a tourist attraction and serves as a staging base for climbers who want to scale Mount Mulange or those who want to explore the countyside at its base. Residential and camping accommodation as is available

Mulanje is aslo the headquarters of the conversation group, the Mulanje Mountain Conservation Trust. The small town of Mulanje is the center of Malawi's tea growing industry and home to The Lujeri Tea Estate, adjacent to the border with Mozambique.

Kalonga

Set on the shores of Lake Malawi, Kalonga is one of Malawi's most northen townships and a palce of growing interest. It has an Interesting but turbulent history as a center for the nineteeth century slave trade and later, as the site of First War battle between German-ruled Tanzania(Kown as Tanganyika at the tiome) and British-ruled Malawi(Nyasalasnd at the time). The town's cemetery has several military graves from the battle.

Kalonga is the largest town before reaching Tanzania border and so a useful place to re-stock abd re-fuel for those on multi-country itinerary.

The town is home to the Cultural and Meseum Centre, Kalonga which covers cultural history and the recent archeological finds of dinosaur and hominid bones. The main exhibition of 'Malawi from Dinosaur of Democracy' includes a remarkable life-sized replica of the 12 m long and 3 m high, 120 million year old Malawisaurus skeleton found in the Kalonga district neary 100 years ago. And a replica of the world's oldest known fossil from mankind's very own Homo genus - a jawone of Homo redolfensis, found slightly to the south near Chilumba.

Chipoka is one of the key ports oth lake where most Lake vessels stop including the MV Ilala. Salima is aweek end treat foe many from Lilongwe and also popular for day visits.
